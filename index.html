1: PURPOSE

To establish standardized guidelines for the safe, effective, and professional operation of LSPD Air-1 in support of ground units, pursuits, searches, surveillance, and emergency response within One Life.

2: Scope

Applies to all certified LSPD pilots assigned to Air-1.

3: Authority & Certification

Only members with LSPD Aviation Certification may pilot a Helicopter.

4: General Rules

  • Air-1 shall maintain a professional and calm demeanor on both air and main radio channels at all times.
  • No low flying over highly populated areas unless tactically necessary.
  • No hovering directly above civilian players for extended periods.
  • Do not engage in pursuits or spotlight players without ground unit request or probable cause.
  • When going on Duty as Air-1 you should announce in radio "Air-1 is up and in the air, and available."
  • Do not track random civilians without reasonable suspicion and ground unit request.
  • Do not Land the Helicopter in the Middle of the Road for no reason.

5: Radio Commutation Standards

  • Use proper phonetic alphabet and 10-codes when applicable.
  • Standard call outs:
    • "Air-1 is overhead [location/incident] with eyes on."
    • "Suspect is [direction of travel], heading [north/south/etc.] at high speed."
    • "Vehicle is now FOOTER, heading [direction]."

6: Pursuit Support Protocol

  • Ground unit must request Air-1 before joining a pursuit.
  • Air-1 becomes primary unit once overhead and assumes direction of pursuit.
  • Provide continuous location, direction, speed, and traffic conditions.
  • Announce when suspect bails - Suspect is now on foot.
  • If pursuit is called off by ground command, Air-1 will break off unless directed otherwise.

7: Thermal Usage

  • Thermals are only permitted when:
    • REQUEST BEFORE USING THERMALS
    • Suspect enters water (Must Request)
    • Clean chase is broken (Must Request)
    • Gang Raids (Must Request)
    • Open Scene Shootouts (Must Request)
Violating this SOP will lead to your Termination of the Aviation Unit
